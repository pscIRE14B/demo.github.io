Mark Zuckerberg

Enginner & Youtuber

Mark Zuckerberg is the co-founder and CEO of Meta (formerly Facebook), one of the world's largest social media platforms. Born in 1984, Zuckerberg launched Facebook in 2004 while attending Harvard University, aiming to connect students online. The platform quickly expanded, becoming a global phenomenon with billions of users. Zuckerberg is known for his innovative vision and sometimes controversial leadership style, particularly in handling issues around privacy, misinformation, and monopolistic practices. Under his leadership, Meta has expanded into areas like virtual reality and the metaverse, positioning itself at the forefront of digital communication and immersive technologies.

Mark Zuckerberg attended Phillips Exeter Academy, excelling in academics and computer programming. He then enrolled at Harvard University in 2002, where he studied psychology and computer science. During his sophomore year, he launched Facebook from his dorm room. The success of the platform led him to drop out of Harvard in 2004 to focus on Facebook full-time.

Mark Zuckerberg co-founded Facebook in 2004, growing it into a global social media giant. As CEO of Meta (formerly Facebook), he has overseen its expansion into platforms like Instagram, WhatsApp, and virtual reality, transforming digital communication and social networking worldwide.
